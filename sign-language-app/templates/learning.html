ASL Alphabet: Letters A-I

Learn the American Sign Language alphabet for letters A through I. Each card shows the proper hand position and provides tips for forming the sign correctly. Once you feel comfortable, head over to the Practice section to test your skills with our AI recognition tool.

A

How to sign "A"

Make a fist with your hand. Extend your thumb to the side of your fist. Make sure your thumb is not crossed over your fingers.

Tip: Keep your palm facing outward, away from your body.
B

How to sign "B"

Hold your hand flat with your fingers and thumb together. Your palm should be facing outward, away from your body.

Tip: Keep your fingers straight and pressed together.
C

How to sign "C"

Curve your fingers and thumb to form a "C" shape. Your palm should be facing to the side.

Tip: Keep your hand relaxed but maintain the curved shape.
D

How to sign "D"

Form a circle with your thumb and index finger, while keeping your other fingers straight up. Your palm should be facing forward.

Tip: The circle should be complete, with your thumb and index finger touching.
E

How to sign "E"

Curl your fingers inward so that your fingertips touch the palm of your hand. Your thumb should be tucked against your palm.

Tip: The fingertips should be visible, touching the palm near the base of the thumb.
F

How to sign "F"

Touch your thumb and index finger together to form a circle. Your other three fingers should be extended upward.

Tip: Keep your middle, ring, and pinky fingers close together and extended.
G

How to sign "G"

Point your index finger forward with your thumb extended to the side. Your other fingers should be tucked into your palm.

Tip: Your thumb and index finger should form an "L" shape when viewed from the side.
H

How to sign "H"

Extend your index and middle fingers out, keeping them together. Your thumb should cross over your pinky and ring finger as they press against your palm.

Tip: Keep your two extended fingers straight and parallel to each other.
I

How to sign "I"

Make a fist and extend only your pinky finger upward. Your thumb can rest alongside your fingers or across them.

Tip: Keep your pinky straight and pointing upward.
